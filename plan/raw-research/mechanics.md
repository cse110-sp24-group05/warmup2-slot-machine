## Algorithm Principle
* **RNG (Random Number Generator):** Mostly uses the RNG algorithm. Each spin is an independent event. Theoretically, whether the previous spin resulted in a jackpot or a losing streak has no effect on the probability of winning in the next spin. Industry standards prohibit intentionally creating “near-miss” outcomes to mislead players after the RNG has determined the result.
* **Volatility / Variability:** Determines the risk level of the game’s math model. High volatility leads to infrequent but large payouts, while low volatility leads to frequent but smaller payouts.

## Common Symbols
* **Payline symbols:** Standard game icons used to form winning combinations.
* **Wild:** Can substitute for some standard symbols to complete winning lines.
* **Scatter:** Typically does not need to appear on the same payline and is often used to trigger free games.
* **Cash / Coin / Hold & Spin symbols:** Icons that display cash or bonus values directly.
* **Progressive / Jackpot symbols:** Related to fixed or cumulative prize pools.
* **Bonus symbols:** Like scatters but are used to trigger secondary minigames or pick-em features.
* **Multiplier symbols:** Standalone symbols that multiply the payout when they are present.

## Paylines
* **Traditional machines:** Fixed paylines, such as 3-reel slots and multi-line machines; a win is only counted if symbols appear on an “active line.”
* **Modern slots:** Feature expanded formats, primarily:
  * **Fixed multi-line paylines:** e.g., 20, 50, or 100 lines; players can choose how many lines to activate (more active lines = more winning combinations covered, but higher total bet).
  * **Ways to win (all-ways system):** Removes fixed lines; as long as matching symbols appear on consecutive reels (usually left to right), it counts as a win, regardless of exact position.
  * **Cluster Pays:** Removes traditional lines and ways. Wins are awarded when certain symbols appear in a contiguous row or column in grid-based games.
  * **Pay both ways:** Allows winning combinations to be formed from right to left as well as the standard left to right.

## Win Conditions
Rules vary by machine, but the core logic is that “a win is awarded when a symbol combination meets the specified conditions.” Common examples include:
* **Payline Wins:** A specific symbol combination appears on a valid payline (usually requiring a continuous sequence from left to right).
* **Ways to Win (non-fixed payline gameplay):** A win is achieved whenever matching symbols appear on adjacent reels, regardless of specific payline positions.
* **Scatter Trigger:** Reaching a certain number triggers free spins, without requiring symbols to be on the same payline.
* **Bonus Trigger:** Entering a special mini-game (such as a wheel of fortune, pick-and-win, Hold & Spin, etc.) to earn additional rewards.
* **Jackpot Trigger:** Winning a grand prize (fixed or progressive) through specific symbols or a progressive mechanism.

## Odds
Each machine has its own mathematical model; there is no uniform probability.
* **RTP (Return to Player):** This represents the average return percentage after long-term betting (e.g., 95% ≈ 95% long-term return, but short-term fluctuations are significant).
* **Hit Frequency:** The expected percentage of spins that will land a winning combination. A 25% hit rate means a player is expected to win something 1 out of every 4 turns on average.

## Modern Features
* **Free Spins:** Continue playing without wagering after triggering.
* **Multipliers:** Winning amounts are multiplied (e.g., x2, x5).
* **Expanding / Sticky Symbols:** Symbols expand in size or remain in place for multiple spins.
* **Hold & Spin:** Lock winning symbols in place and spin the remaining positions.
* **Progressive Jackpot:** A portion of players’ bets contributes to the prize pool, causing the jackpot to grow over time.
* **Cascading Reels:** After a win, symbols disappear, and new ones fall from above to continue the calculation.
* **Megaways:** The number of symbols per column varies, resulting in a constantly changing number of winning combinations.
* **Bonus Buy / Feature Drop:** Allows players to pay a premium (like 100x their base bet) to bypass the main game and skip straight to the minigame.
* **Gamble Feature:** Lets players risk their payout in a 50/50 minigame to double their win.
