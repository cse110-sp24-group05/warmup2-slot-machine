## Gambling:
**Name:** Mason Allin (new)
**Age:** 20

**Background:**
- Second-year college student studying Business. Grew up in a family where gambling is normalized—relatives often talk about wins, show off earnings, and treat it like a smart way to make money. Mason has never seriously gambled before but has seen enough success stories to believe it works.

**Why they'd play a browser slot game:** 
- He believes this is a low-risk way to test gambling without going to a casino. Initially, he treats it like a “trial run” before potentially moving to real-money platforms.
- It’s convenience for him since he is still in school, so with online he can play it anywhere he wants without having to go to casino
His friends around him have part time jobs and earn money. He wants to make money too, but does not want to go to work so playing slot game can help him earn money without having to go to work
- Easy and low-commitment at first, no ID checks

**Care about:** 
- Prizes: he wants a quick win with actually real money
- Money: maybe a small amount of money at first, but consistent. Like everytime he plays or maybe once a week get at least 100+. Then, a jackpots or large rewards
- Ranking: to show that he’s good at the game, not just lucky
- Progress: he wants to see that the more he plays, the closer he gets to winning big, it makes him want to play everyday

**Quit?:** 
- Losing early because he wants quick win to see that it actually works
- The game is just random, not tactically
- Feeling scammed
- No visible progress or winning
- VIf he sees the downfall/consequences in the relatives. Like if the relative goes bankruptcy because of this, he will quit

**Quote:** “I’m close. I just need one good win to prove it works to make my parents stop complaining to find a job.”


**Name:** Jack Potts (professional)
**Age:** 45

**Background:**
- Jack has been gambling for over 20 years. He started in his 20s with sports betting and casino games, eventually becoming highly experienced with odds, patterns, and bankroll management. He treats gambling as a serious side income, not just entertainment. He has a stable job but spends a significant portion of his time gambling, often believing he has developed an “edge” over time. He believes that it is a long-term financial development. Take his salary to make more money from it

**Why they'd play a browser slot game:**
- Convenience: he can play and work at the same time, do not need to spend his all day in casino
- Habit because he plays for over years
- A side job to get more money
- He feels like he has experienced with gambling so it will give him an advantage to win more money, even though it’s random
privacy, since he has a job, he does not want his boss or his businesses partners to know
- Comfort because he can relax with this after a long day at work
  
**Care about:**
- Status: see as successful or skilled. He wants his name on the top with dedication to show that in order to get this high, he commits to it for many years. And, he is skilled so no one can get over him. He knows how the game works
- High value prizes: large payouts or jackpots because he sees this as his second job. To satisfy his ego, he spends time and risk on it so he needs it to be worth it
- Fairness of the game: no rigged because he spends lots of time playing so he knows the technique.
- Control & efficiency: He prefers fast gameplay and the ability to play continuously. The more control he has over pacing and volume of play, the more he feels in control of outcomes.
  
**Quit?:** 
- Financial consequences: personal financial loss or debt
- Get controlled by the games, make him lose more and make impulsive decisions
- Personal relationships: his addiction of game begins to harm his family such as borrowing money 
- Professional risks: if his boss or his businesses partners find out 
- His belief that he is professional. When he keeps on losing money, break his belief that he has years of experience so he cannot lose, self-esteem gets crushed
  
**Quote:** "There’s no way I will lose, I've been doing this for years.”



## Entertainment: 
**Name:** Steven Horkings (new player)

**Age:** 26

**Background:** Junior graphic designer that’s still living with his parents. Grew up watching his parents play scratch off tickets occasionally but never really found an appeal to gambling. Gaming experience is limited to Candy Crush and Piano Tiles. Picked up a browser slot game after a coworker mentioned playing one during lunch break and laughed about a big “virtual” win. While is still skeptical about the idea about gambling, he plays the game from time to time as he finds joy in winning virtual prizes that don’t require real money investment.

**Why they'd play a browser slot game:** Low commitment as he doesn't want to download anything or doesn't want to create an account with his information and most importantly, doesn't want to use his credit card. Browser slot game is a quick turnaround game that he can play for a few minutes and close the tab whenever he’s done with it, without the feeling of he has committed to something. 

**Care about:** 
- Visuals and UX: Wants the game to look good and stylish
- No paywall required to play: If a paywall or “buy tokens” hits him in the first two minutes, he’s out
- Clarity: He wants to understand the rules and prizes clearly. If he feels like it’s a confusing mess, he will lose interest.
- Quick sessions: spinning should feel snappy, not a 6 second animation everytime
  
**Quit?:** 
- Aggressive monetization: pay to continue or ads every 3 spins
- Slow or laggy animations
- Requiring an account/email to setup or play
- Ugly or cluttered UI, he’s a designer, he knows best
  
**Quote:** “I am not trying to win anything, I just want to kill some time by not being on social media”


**Name:** Frodo Baggins

**Age:** 44

**Background:** Semi retired HVAC technician. Loves to play online video games. Has been playing casual slot-style games for a decade. Started with ‘Slotomania’ back in the mid 2010s and now currently bounces between three or four browser slot games that he bookmarked. Tried real money online casinos twice but didn’t really like them as the stakes made it stressful instead of relaxing. Sometimes he’ll drop like $5 on a coin pack if a game has earned it but it's rare as he treats it like tipping a bartender, not gambling. 

**Why they'd play a browser slot game:** Routine and relaxation. Frodo plays the game usually in the evening with TV on in the background, usually during ad breaks in football games. Over ten years, he has developed an appreciation for the feel of a good slot machine, the rhythm, the sound design and the suspense and anticipation. He knows the math isn’t in his favor most of the time but he couldn’t care less as there’s no real money on the line. He also likes chasing in-game progress such as unlocking achievements or levelling up. 
**Care about:** 
- Fairness: He’s played enough games to understand when a game feels rigged against him. He’ll catch on a bad RTP in a week.
- Variety of machines & themes: one slot gets boring real fast, He wants a library to rotate through.
- Progression system: daily bonus, level up system, collectibles, leaderboards. Gives him a reason to come back tomorrow.
- Respectful monetization: Willing to spend some if the game treats him righ
- Sound design: Huge for him. Satisfying reel stops and coin drop sounds are an experience itself.
  
**Quit?:** 
- Rigged mechanics
- Pay to win progression where free players can’t unlock new contents
- Aggressive monetization: ads every few spins
- Small game library with no new content over time
- Account / server issues: if his progress vanishes, he’s not coming back
  
**Quote:** “I’ve been spinning reels since Obama’s second term. I know a fair game when I see one, and I know when I’m being hustled. And I don’t stick around for the hustle.”
